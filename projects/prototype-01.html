Context

T-PREP/PREP is an on-campus summer bridge program run by Engineering Student Services (ESS) designed to help incoming junior-transfer/freshmen students transition into the rigorous academic and research environment of Berkeley Engineering. The design component puts together teams to take on an immersive prototyping challenge (with the SPRINT design process) hosted at the Jacobs Institute for Design Innovation to create a product that can benefit the world. This all culminates at the last day of the program with the PREP & T-PREP Maker Design Expo, where all 24 teams present their projects to the cohort and an industry panel in hopes of being one of five award winners. I was grateful to have been accepted into the program following an application and looked forward to taking on the opportunity.

Overview

Around 8% of college students have reported experiencing a spiked drink, and women are more likely to be affected, with one survey reporting that 15% of women have experienced drink spiking before. Current solutions include testing strips, coasters, and cup covers, but these options can be inconvenient. They often rely on people remembering to bring them from home or actively choosing to use them in the moment. SpikeGuard approaches the problem differently by combining drink-testing stirring sticks with a secure dispenser system, making the product easier to access and use directly at events, bars, parties, and other social spaces.

The idea is built around stirring sticks that can test for drink tampering while staying in the drink, allowing for more constant monitoring compared to one-time-use testing strips. The dispenser would make the sticks available on-site, while the final design would include a locked lid and paywall to keep the product secure and controlled. SpikeGuard stands out because it combines multiple tests into one product, can be carried in the drink unlike many current drug tests, and is designed to be user-friendly while still being secure. Compared to regular dispensers, SpikeGuard would offer stronger protection through a more controlled design. The long-term goal for SpikeGuard is to manufacture both drink tests and physical barriers in-house, sell the dispensers, and continue supplying refills. With this model, SpikeGuard could scale across the country and become a common safety tool in social spaces. As one UC Berkeley fraternity president shared, “If DrinkGuard was on the market we would definitely invest in it to keep our members and visitors safe during our events.”

During the project's timeline, I had the privilege to be advised by Professor Scott Moura (Engineering) and Master’s Student Shikha Shah (Design).
